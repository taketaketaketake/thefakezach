For those of you that don't know, my professional background is in video production. I've spent over a decade producing and creating videos for clients. That's actually what most people know me for professionally.

I'm thankful for every client, every referral, and every opportunity I've had to do what I once loved.

I'm done. Everybody I know is cheap and unprofessional.

The name of the game in business is word-of-mouth and referrals. I've been around long enough where I regularly get lots of referrals and business through word-of-mouth. The problem is cheap people recommend cheap people.

Furthermore, people don't have any value for free work regardless of what it is. They did not pay for it, so there is little incentive to care about it.

Now, do I make myself unavailable to these people? Do I bluntly explain that I'm no longer interested in doing favors?

Either way, this thought came to mind as I'm getting ready to leave my kids' dance class and go do free video work for a project I don't even really know about.

Consider this a timestamp and announcement of where I stand on free work.
