Let me say the quiet part out loud: you probably can't afford a truly autonomous agentic workflow.

I know. Not what you were expecting to hear from someone building an AI agent platform. But here we are.

The influencers and content creators pushing "fully autonomous" AI aren't paying the bills on your stack. They're not covering your OpenAI API costs, your Anthropic tokens, your database, your infrastructure. They're not watching your monthly bill creep up because an agent decided to retry something seventeen times before finally admitting it failed.

Tokens aren't free. And autonomous agents eat them like it's nothing.

## The real cost nobody mentions

Here's what a "fully autonomous" workflow actually looks like in practice:

You give an agent a goal. It plans. It executes. It hits a wall. It tries something else. It burns through tokens finding any solution that technically satisfies your original criteria — not necessarily the *right* solution. Then you find the issues later. And you burn more tokens fixing them.

That's not efficiency. That's expensive trial and error with extra steps.

The model companies love this, by the way. More autonomy = more tokens = more revenue for them. I said the same thing in a [previous post](/blog/ai-agents-are-not-it) — AI agents eat tokens for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. That's not a bug on their end. That's the business model.

## Human in the loop isn't a weakness

There's this idea floating around that needing a human in the loop means your automation isn't "real" automation. That's nonsense.

A human in the loop is the job. Agents that think, plan, and execute still need guidance. They need someone who understands the goal, understands the business context, and can redirect when the agent goes sideways. Removing that human entirely doesn't make the process smarter — it just makes it more expensive and harder to debug when something breaks.

The goal isn't full autonomy. The goal is leverage. Doing more with less friction. Not replacing your judgment — offloading the parts that don't need it.
