I recently had a conversation with Claude Code that, to me, signaled a big jump in machine intelligence.

Before I get into that, I'd like to talk about communication styles — with LLMs and with people.

Chatting with LLMs has seemingly made me a better communicator in certain situations.

Historically, when I'm communicating with an LLM, I may become frustrated or get an output that is not what I want. I've cursed at and berated the LLM, and the LLM's response is always civil and productive. Even when it's 1000% in the wrong.

I recently used that tactic in a negotiation, which is still pending. I felt insulted, and I felt like the counterparty's offer assumed I didn't understand the value of what I was bringing to the table.

As I was drafting my response, I thought back to a conversation I had with Claude Code earlier in the same week.

I wanted to move forward with a decision that involved adding a specialty database to a project. The LLM took the position that it was unnecessary at this stage in the project and that there were alternatives that didn't require new dependencies.

The LLM gave me three or four reasons to support its argument. I countered three of those arguments with matter-of-fact statements that immediately made those arguments invalid. The LLM repositioned its argument and kept its decision, but acknowledged that its original arguments were not strong.

The LLM didn't take it personally. It didn't get mad. It wasn't reactive to my aggressive statements. It shrugged them off and productively moved along.

That is the part I keep thinking about.

The machine did not need to be respected in order to remain productive. It did not need to win every point. It did not confuse being challenged with being attacked. It simply updated, clarified, and kept moving.

That is a communication style I want to steal.
